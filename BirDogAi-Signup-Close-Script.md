# BirDogAi — Signup &amp; Close Scripts

The exact words to use the moment a prospect says "okay, how do I sign up?" Don't improvise. Don't fumble. Closing happens in 60 seconds or it doesn't happen.

---

## BEFORE the meetup — set this up once

You need three things ready on your phone or laptop before any meetup:

1. **Stripe Payment Link** for each tier (free to create at stripe.com — takes 10 minutes)
   - Founding Member: $97/mo recurring
   - Founding Pro: $297/mo recurring
   - Founding Syndicator: $797/mo recurring
   - Annual versions of each ($970, $2,970, $7,970)
2. **Short URLs or QR codes** for each link (use bit.ly free or a QR generator) — print 4 small QR codes you can hand someone to scan
3. **A simple Founding Member Agreement PDF** (one page, plain language, signed digitally via DocuSign free tier or just email-and-sign) confirming the locked-rate-forever promise. Without this, you'll forget which customer locked which rate.

---

## THE CLOSE — when they say "okay, how do I sign up?"

This is your moment. The conversation has shifted from interest to commitment. Move fast and stay calm.

### Step 1 — Confirm the tier (15 seconds)

> "Awesome. Quick question — how many deals are you actively looking at in a typical month?"

Their answer tells you which tier to recommend:

- **0-3 deals/month → Founding Member ($97)**
  > "Founding Member is right for you then. $97 a month, locked forever. You get 3 full analyses per month, all 7 capabilities, and email support. Sound good?"

- **4+ deals/month, solo investor → Founding Pro ($297)**
  > "Pro is the move. $297 a month, locked forever. Unlimited analyses, 5 markets, and a 30-min call with me every month to walk through your pipeline. Most investors with your volume are on Pro. Sound good?"

- **Syndicator, fund manager, or anyone managing LPs → Founding Syndicator ($797)**
  > "Syndicator tier was built for what you're doing. $797 a month, locked forever. Everything in Pro plus the LP relations module — distribution waterfalls, capital calls, monthly LP updates drafted automatically. We cap this tier at 5 founding members; you'd be one of them. Sound good?"

### Step 2 — Offer the annual option (10 seconds)

> "One more thing — you can pay monthly, or pay annually and save the equivalent of 2 months. Annual is $X,XXX. Most founding members do annual because the rate's locked anyway. Either work for you?"

Do NOT pressure. Whichever they pick is fine. Annual gives you cash up front but monthly converts faster.

### Step 3 — Take payment NOW (30 seconds)

This is the most important step. Do not say "I'll send you an invoice tomorrow." Don't.

Pull out your phone. Open the appropriate Stripe payment link.

> "Cool. I've got the link right here on my phone — easiest way is for you to scan this QR code and put your card in. Takes about 30 seconds. While you're doing that, I'll grab your email to send you the welcome doc and the founding member agreement."

Hand them the QR code or your phone. Stay quiet. Let them complete it.

If their phone is in their hand: text them the link directly.
> "What's your number? I'll text you the link right now."

If they hesitate or say "I'll do it tonight":
> "Totally fair. The only thing is the founding rate is tied to today — once we hit the cap on this tier, it goes to standard pricing. I can hold the spot for you for 24 hours if you want to lock it in now and finish the payment when you get home. Want me to do that?"

(That's a real promise — actually hold the spot. Don't lie.)

### Step 4 — Confirm and welcome (15 seconds)

The moment Stripe shows the green checkmark on their phone:

> "Done. Welcome to BirDogAi, [name]. You're founding member number [N] of [25/10/5]. Couple things happen now: I'll email you the welcome doc and your founding member agreement in the next hour. We'll set up a 30-min onboarding call this week to dial in your buy-box. And starting tomorrow morning you can send me deals to analyze.
>
> Anything you want me to look at this week? Send it over tonight and I'll have the report to you by Tuesday."

Shake their hand. Move on. Don't oversell after the close — every word after "done" is a chance to introduce buyer's remorse.

## OBJECTION HANDLING — the 6 things you'll actually hear

### "Let me think about it / talk to my partner / sleep on it"

> "Totally fair. The reason I'm asking now is the founding rate caps at [25/10/5] members and disappears once we move to standard pricing. If you want, I can hold your spot for 24 hours — no card required — while you decide. After that I'd have to give it to the next person on the list. Want me to hold it?"

Either they say yes (and you follow up the next day) or they tell you the real objection. Both are wins.

### "How do I know it actually works?"

> "Totally fair question. Best way to find out is to send me a deal you're looking at right now and I'll run a full analysis on it free this week. You'll see exactly what you'd be getting every month. If it's useful, we can talk about signing up after. If it's not, no harm done."

(This is your free-deal-analysis trojan horse. Use it.)

### "$297 / $797 is too expensive"

> "I get it. Quick math: if BirDog catches one bad deal you would've otherwise overpaid on by $10K, it's paid for itself for the next 30+ months. The Founding Member tier at $97 has a 3-deal cap which fits a lot of investors fine — want me to walk you through that one instead?"

Down-tier them rather than discount. Discounting below stated price destroys your pricing forever.

### "I already use [PropStream / DealMachine / Roofstock / etc.]"

> "Cool — those give you data lists. BirDog gives you decisions: full underwriting, contract review, seller outreach drafted. They're complementary, not competitors. Most of our founding members keep PropStream and add BirDog on top. Want to see one of your PropStream leads run through BirDog?"

### "Can I just buy single analyses without a subscription?"

> "Right now we're focused on founding members only — that's where the locked rate and direct support come from. If a subscription doesn't fit, the free analysis offer still stands. But honestly, most investors who try one analysis end up wanting it for every deal they look at, which is why we built the subscription."

(Don't offer per-deal pricing. It cannibalizes your subscription tiers.)

### "What happens if BirDog goes out of business?"

> "Fair concern with any startup. Three things: cancel anytime no friction, your data exports cleanly anytime, and I'm a real estate investor myself — BirDog is the tool I built for my own portfolio first. Even if every customer left tomorrow, I'd keep building it because I need it."

## AFTER the close — the first 48 hours

This is where most founders drop the ball. Treat your first founding members like the asset they are.

**Within 1 hour:** Send the welcome email.

> Subject: Welcome, founding member [N] of [25] — what's next
>
> [name] —
>
> Welcome aboard. You're founding member [N] of [25] — your $[97/297/797]/month rate is locked for the life of your subscription, in writing.
>
> Three things happening this week:
>
> 1. **Founding Member Agreement** — attached as PDF. Sign and send back when you have a sec.
> 2. **Onboarding call** — 30 minutes, this week. Pick a slot: [calendly link]
> 3. **Send me a deal** — anything you're looking at right now, just reply with the address. I'll have a full BirDog analysis to you within 24 hours.
>
> Excited to have you in. Talk soon.
>
> — [your name]

**Within 24 hours:** Run their first deal analysis. Make it your best work. Their first impression of the product is the first deliverable.

**Within 7 days:** Do the onboarding call. Capture their buy-box, their markets, their pain points. This is your most important conversation with them — it shapes everything you build for the next 6 months.

**Within 30 days:** Ask them for a testimonial AND for one introduction to another investor. Your first 25 founding members should each refer 1-2 more. That's how you fill the cap without spending a dollar on marketing.

When you hit 25 Founding Members, 10 Pro, and 5 Syndicator — you close enrollment, raise prices, and you have a real business with $20K+ in MRR before you've spent a dollar on cold email.
